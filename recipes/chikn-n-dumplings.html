Chikn & Dumplings

This is my ultimate savory comfort dish! It is not quick nor easy, but cooking at a relaxed and unhurried pace, at least for me, is part of what makes comfort food relaxing and enjoyable.

Makes 4 servings

  1. Soak about 10 minutes until soft:

    • 1 - 1 1/2 cups soy curls
    • 2 cups hot water
    • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce

  2. In a large pot, saute the following until soft, about 5 minutes:

    • 2 tbsp olive oil or vegan butter
    • 2 tbsp red palm oil
    • 1 small yellow or white onion, chopped
    • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
    • 2 ribs celery, chopped
    • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1/4 tsp black pepper

  3. Drain and squeeze out the soy curls and add to the pot, letting them get brown and crispy.

  4. Stir in flour until coated, about 1 minute. Mix broth together and slowly add to pot a little at a time, stirring constantly between additions:

    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 4 cups hot vegetable stock
    • 2 tbsp vegan chikn bouillon base

  5. Add to pot and cook covered on medium-low heat 5-10 minutes while you prepare the dumpling dough:

    • 2 tsp sugar
    • 1 bay leaf

  6. Whisk the dries and wets separately. Combine until a shaggy dough forms and set aside:

    • Dries:
      • 1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
      • 2 tsp baking powder
      • 1/2 tsp dried rosemary
      • 3/4 tsp sea salt
    • Wets:
      • 2/3 cup full-fat oat milk
      • 1 tbsp melted vegan butter

  7. Stir in:

    • 1/2 cup full-fat oat milk
    • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 tsp dried thyme

  8. Scoop 1-2 tbsp balls of dumpling dough into the stew. Cook covered over medium-low heat for 15-20 minutes.

  9. Enjoy!
